Biryani

Biryani is a famous South Asian rice dish made with fragrant basmati rice, aromatic spices, and meat or vegetables. It is believed to have originated from the Mughal kitchens and has many regional variations across India.

The dish is prepared by layering partially cooked rice with marinated meat such as chicken, mutton, or beef. Spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, and saffron are used to create its rich aroma and flavor.

The marination typically includes yogurt, ginger, garlic, chili, and a blend of traditional spices. The layered mixture is then cooked on low heat using a method called “dum,” which allows the flavors to blend together slowly.

Biryani is often served with raita, salad, or boiled eggs. It is popular for celebrations, festivals, and special occasions due to its rich taste and satisfying nature.
